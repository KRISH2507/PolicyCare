POLICY DOCUMENT
===============
Policy Name:   CareShield Basic
Insurer:       SafeGuard General Insurance Co. Ltd.
Policy Type:   Individual Health Insurance
Document Ref:  SG-CSB-2024-v1

---

OVERVIEW
--------
CareShield Basic is an entry-level individual health insurance plan designed for
first-time buyers and low-income households seeking essential hospitalisation cover
at an affordable annual premium. It provides straightforward inpatient coverage
without complex riders or add-ons.

---

PREMIUM & COVER
---------------
Annual Premium:    ₹6,500 (age 18–35) | ₹9,200 (age 36–50) | ₹14,800 (age 51–60)
Sum Insured:       ₹3,00,000 (Three Lakh Rupees)
Policy Term:       1 year, renewable annually
Grace Period:      30 days from renewal due date

---

ELIGIBILITY
-----------
Minimum Entry Age:  18 years
Maximum Entry Age:  60 years
Renewability:       Lifetime, subject to policy terms

---

INCLUSIONS (WHAT IS COVERED)
-----------------------------
- Inpatient hospitalisation for illness or accidental injury (minimum 24-hour admission)
- Pre- and post-hospitalisation expenses: 30 days before and 60 days after discharge
- Day-care procedures listed in the approved schedule (112 procedures)
- Ambulance charges: up to ₹1,500 per hospitalisation event
- Organ donor expenses: up to ₹50,000 (donor's medical costs only)
- AYUSH treatment (Ayurveda, Yoga, Unani, Siddha, Homeopathy): up to ₹20,000/year
  at government-recognised hospitals only

---

EXCLUSIONS (WHAT IS NOT COVERED)
----------------------------------
- Pre-existing diseases (PED) during the first 4 years of continuous coverage
- Any illness diagnosed within the first 30 days of policy inception (initial waiting period)
- Specific diseases in Year 1 and Year 2: cataracts, hernia, joint replacement,
  varicose veins, kidney stones, piles, fistula, sinusitis
- Cosmetic or aesthetic surgery and treatments
- Dental treatment (unless arising from accidental injury requiring hospitalisation)
- Spectacles, contact lenses, hearing aids
- Maternity and newborn expenses
- Self-inflicted injuries, suicide attempts
- War, nuclear, biological, or chemical hazard events
- Experimental or unproven treatments
- Obesity treatment and weight-loss surgery
- Infertility and assisted reproduction

---

WAITING PERIODS
---------------
Initial Waiting Period:         30 days (all illnesses except accidents)
Pre-existing Disease (PED):     4 years of continuous coverage
Specific Disease Waiting Period: 2 years (listed conditions above)

Note: The 4-year PED waiting period is the longest among comparable plans in this
segment. Policyholders with diabetes, hypertension, or cardiac conditions should
carefully evaluate this before purchasing.

---

CO-PAY
------
No co-pay for policyholders aged below 60.
20% co-pay applies for policyholders aged 60 and above at the time of claim.

---

ROOM RENT SUB-LIMIT
-------------------
Room rent capped at ₹1,500 per day (single standard room).
ICU charges capped at ₹3,000 per day.
If a higher category room is chosen, proportionate deduction applies to all
associated charges (surgeon fees, anaesthesia, etc.).

---

SUB-LIMITS ON SPECIFIC PROCEDURES
-----------------------------------
Cataract surgery:         ₹20,000 per eye
Knee/hip replacement:     ₹80,000 per joint
Dialysis (per session):   ₹1,500

---

CLAIM TYPE
----------
Cashless: Available at 4,200+ network hospitals across India.
Reimbursement: Submit original bills within 15 days of discharge.

---

NETWORK HOSPITALS
-----------------
Primarily Tier-2 and Tier-3 city hospitals. Metro coverage is limited.
Policyholders in metro cities may find fewer cashless options compared to
mid-tier plans. Always verify network status before admission.

---

NO-CLAIM BONUS
--------------
5% increase in sum insured for each claim-free year, up to a maximum of 25%.
Bonus is reset to base sum insured if a claim is made.

---

RENEWAL NOTE
------------
Premium increases at ages 36, 46, and 56. Policyholders are advised to review
coverage adequacy at each renewal milestone, particularly if health conditions
have changed.

---

SUITABILITY SUMMARY
--------------------
Best suited for:
  - Young adults (18–30) with no pre-existing conditions seeking basic cover
  - Residents of Tier-2 and Tier-3 cities where hospital costs are lower
  - Individuals with annual income below ₹3 lakh who need affordable entry-level cover

Not recommended for:
  - Individuals with diabetes, hypertension, or other pre-existing conditions
    (4-year waiting period is a significant gap in coverage)
  - Metro city residents who require higher room rent limits
  - Families needing maternity or OPD benefits
